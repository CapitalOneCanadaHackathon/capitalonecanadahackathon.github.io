2019 Capital One Digital for Good Tech Jam Participant Agreement

The Capital One Digital for Good Tech Jam (the "Tech Jam") is administered by Hackworks Inc. ("Hackworks") on behalf of Capital One Bank (Canada Branch) ("Capital One") (Hackworks and Capital One are referred to collectively herein as the "Organizers") for the benefit of participating non-profit organizations (each, a "Charity" and collectively, the “Charities”). By registering for and participating in the Tech Jam, you, the registrant ("you"; and "your") confirm you have reviewed and agree to this Participant Agreement (the "Agreement") between you and the Organizers, and will abide by the instructions and decisions of the Organizers and the event staff.

1. PURPOSE:

The Tech Jam is an opportunity for you to innovate, ideate, and rapidly prototype a creative technological solution to a unique challenge faced by a Canadian non-profit organization. The Tech Jam is a charitable event with no prizes or compensation other than your participation and is not a competition or a contest.

2. ELIGIBILITY:

To participate in the Tech Jam, you must be at least the age of majority in your jurisdiction of residence. You must be also be a legal resident of Canada, unless you are an employee or contractor of Capital One, its parent company, and their respective subsidiaries and affiliates, in which case, you must be a resident of Canada or of the United States. The Tech Jam is an invite-only event; therefore, you must have been received an invitation from the Organizers to be eligible to participate.

The Organizers may at any time in their sole discretion disqualify any Tech Jam participant (each, a “Participant”) who does not meet the Tech Jam's eligibility criteria, fails to comply with this Agreement or the Tech Jam Code of Conduct, or attempts to disrupt the Tech Jam in any manner.

3. REGISTRATION:

To participate, you must register for the Tech Jam through the registration process provided in your invitation from the Organizers. You must complete the registration process, providing accurate and complete information, and accept this Agreement within the time period specified by the Organizers to successfully register.

Once registered, you'll receive a confirmation email from the Organizers at the email address provided on your registration.

4. TEAMS:

Each Participant is required to work on a team of four (4) to eight (8) Participants (each, a "Team"). You can only work on one Team over the course of the Tech Jam, and you can't trade, swap, or re-assign Team members except with the permission of the Organizers. If you haven't formed your Team in advance of the Tech Jam, the Organizers may assign you to one based on your skills, experience, or other factors at the Organizers' sole discretion.

5. TECH JAM:

The Tech Jam begins on November 15, 2019 at 6:00 p.m. EST and ends on November 17, 2019 at 5:00 p.m. EST (the "Tech Jam Period"). The Tech Jam will take place at the Arcadian Loft, 401 Bay St 8th Floor, Toronto, Ontario. You're responsible for transportation, and any associated costs to and from the Tech Jam venue.

Prior to the Tech Jam Period, each Team will be assigned to a Charity. A representative from the Charity will brief your Team on the Charity's objectives, activities, and unique technological challenge (a "Charity Challenge"). The final decision as to Charity and Charity Challenge assignments will be at the discretion of the Organizers. You can't change Teams or obtain a trade or replacement Charity or Charity Challenge, except with the permission of the Organizers.

During the Tech Jam Period, all Teams are invited to create and demonstrate a solution to their assigned Charity Challenge. “Solution” means any and all content created prior to and during the Tech Jam Period to address the Charity Challenge, including source code, object code, and any associated documentation relating to the Solution including technical specifications and instructions for using the Solution, whether in hard copy, electronic, or any other form. Teams may work on their Solutions at any time throughout the Tech Jam Period, but must be prepared to demonstrate, using a live demo, video, screenshots, or other mechanisms by the time and in the format set out in the Tech Jam schedule available at www.digitalforgood.com (the “Website”). Solutions are subject to additional requirements, set out under the section titled "YOUR SOLUTION" below.

You’re required to provide your own computer(s) and any other hardware and software required to develop your Solution. The Organizers and the Charities will make reasonable efforts to provide access to software, APIs, databases, networks, or other tools to assist you in developing your Solution; however, the Organizers aren't obligated to provide you with any hardware or software support. You will be provided with food and drink over the course of the Tech Jam, but are otherwise responsible for any and all costs or expenses you incur in participating in the Tech Jam. If you have any allergies or dietary restrictions, it is your responsibility to inform the Tech Jam staff. If you have a disability and require accommodation, you must notify the Tech Jam staff so that a reasonable accommodation may be made.
